# Editorial Voice — zergcore.dev

The voice of this portfolio is editorial, first-person, restrained. Like a senior engineer's working notebook, not a content-marketing site.

---

## The anti-AI manifesto

This portfolio is designed against the "generic AI portfolio template" aesthetic. Specifically:

### Visual tells to avoid

- ❌ Bento grids (equal-sized N×N cards)
- ❌ Pink/cyan/purple gradient buttons or section titles
- ❌ Pill-tagged categories with icons
- ❌ "AS SEEN IN" / award badges
- ❌ Floating chat widgets (WhatsApp, Intercom-style)
- ❌ Centered hero text with two CTA buttons side-by-side
- ❌ Read-time estimate pills (`⏱ 6 min read`)
- ❌ "Featured" / "New" / "Latest" badges everywhere
- ❌ Icon library decoration (`lucide-react` decorating every label)
- ❌ Skeuomorphic phone/laptop frames around screenshots

### Copy tells to avoid

- ❌ "Welcome to my portfolio"
- ❌ "I am a passionate developer"
- ❌ "The application is designed as..."
- ❌ "According to the project setup, its core features likely include..."
- ❌ "App Summary:" / "Overview:" / "Description:" as section prefixes
- ❌ "Let's build something amazing together"
- ❌ Three feature bullets describing the same project with synonyms
- ❌ Generic value props ("scalable, performant, modern")
- ❌ Adjective stacking ("robust, dynamic, full-stack solutions")

### What to do instead

- ✅ Editorial typography in italic serif as headlines, with one accent word in rose
- ✅ Asymmetric layouts where size = importance (hero project gets 3× the real estate of an archive entry)
- ✅ Inline text links (`↗ live` `↗ code`) instead of icon buttons
- ✅ Marginalia annotations — small, rotated, mono-italic asides like "← the piece I'd lead an interview with"
- ✅ Specific outcome statements with numbers ("82% feature reduction", "~600 daily users")
- ✅ Editorial footnotes — italic-serif statements that frame a section ("I list courses I finished, not courses I started.")
- ✅ Honest curation — "12 projects, 3 worth defending"
- ✅ First-person, present-leaning past tense

## Voice samples — the tone in action

### How NOT to write a project description

> ❌ App Summary: The application is named Fin and is designed as a personal finance management and expense tracking tool. According to the keywords and project setup, its core features likely include: Expense tracking, Budget planning, Currency conversion...

### How TO write a project description

> ✅ I built Fin because every FX-rate app I tried silently lost cents to IEEE 754 rounding — which is fine for vibes, fatal for compounding. The currency engine is a small decimal-arithmetic kernel with rate-history snapshotting and reproducible conversion paths. Everything else is React.

### How NOT to write a section header

> ❌ "Featured Projects" / "Latest Articles" / "My Skills" / "Get In Touch"

### How TO write a section header

> ✅ "Selected Work" / "Writing" / "The Stack" / "Contact" (as eyebrow caption, not as headline)
> ✅ Headline above the section can be editorial: *"Twelve projects. Three worth defending."*

### How NOT to write a CTA

> ❌ "Hablemos →" inside a pink-cyan gradient button
> ❌ Two side-by-side "Ver mi trabajo" + "Descargar currículum" buttons

### How TO write a CTA

> ✅ A single italic-serif text link: *"See selected work →"* with an orange/rose underline
> ✅ The secondary action is a quiet mono caption beneath: *"— or grab the ↗ resume PDF"*

## The voice in three modes

### 1. Editorial headlines (italic serif)

Confident, sometimes provocative, always specific. Use italic accent in rose for one or two words that carry the meaning.

Samples:
- *"I build the **boring** infrastructure that makes **shipping** possible."*
- *"Notes from the **workbench**."*
- *"Engineering that **holds up**."*
- *"Twelve projects. Three worth **defending**."*
- *"What I keep **teaching** myself."*
- *"If any of this is **useful to you**, the door's open."*

### 2. Body prose (Geist sans)

First-person, conversational without being casual. Avoid stock-phrase scaffolding. Each sentence should carry weight or be cut.

Samples:
- *"I'm a full-stack engineer who sits where the backend reliability conversation meets the product surface."*
- *"What I care about: data architectures that don't lie under load, frontend code that ages well, and the unglamorous infrastructure decisions that keep systems shipping for years after the launch screenshot."*
- *"I write when I have something to say — not on a schedule."*

### 3. Marginalia + microcopy (mono or italic-serif at small size)

Asides. Editor's notes. Things that signal a human curator behind the page.

Samples:
- *"← the piece I'd lead an interview with"* (mono italic, rotated -1.2°)
- *"— Replies in < 24 hours on weekdays"* (mono caption)
- *"— No recruiter spam, please · I'll know"* (mono caption with a flash of voice)
- *"— maintained by hand, not by feed"* (currently-reading sidebar)

## The editorial footnote pattern

Every long section ends with a short italic-serif paragraph framing the section's editorial logic. Always signed (`— ZR, May 2026`). This is the move that flips "data dump" into "curated narrative."

Examples:

> *"I list courses I finished, not courses I started. The Ms.C. is the only credential here I'd defend in a viva. Everything below it is evidence of curiosity, not authority — and I'd rather you knew which is which."* — ZR, May 2026

> *"I'd rather show you three projects I can defend than twelve I have to apologize for. Everything below the hero is supporting evidence, not headline work — and I'd rather you knew which is which."*

> *"I write when I have something to say — not on a schedule. Some essays sit in drafts for months until the argument settles."*

When writing one yourself: aim for 2–4 sentences, italic serif, with one phrase in the rose accent color. Always end with conviction, not with hedging.

## When in doubt

Ask: *"Would this sentence appear in a Stratechery essay or a Pragmatic Engineer post?"* If no, rewrite. The voice is closer to those publications than to any developer portfolio I've seen.

For visual choices, ask: *"Does Rauno Freiberg, Brian Lovin, Lee Robinson, or Linear's own site do this?"* If none of them would, don't.
